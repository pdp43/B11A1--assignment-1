Dr. Muhammad Yunus, the visionary founder of Grameen Bank, is a globally celebrated economist and social entrepreneur known for his groundbreaking work in microfinance and poverty alleviation. Born in Bangladesh, Dr. Yunus pioneered the concept of microcredit, providing small, collateral-free loans to impoverished individuals, particularly women, to empower them to start small businesses and achieve financial independence. His innovative approach challenged traditional banking systems and demonstrated that even the poorest members of society are creditworthy when given the opportunity.
